# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, internship coordinators, hackathon collaborators, senior developers, AI builders, and technical classmates who need to understand quickly whether Nachiketh can build useful full-stack software and learn fast. They are often scanning many student portfolios and looking for evidence of ownership, working demos, security awareness, and AI curiosity rather than inflated senior claims.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to position Nachiketh Reddy as a Year 2 IT diploma student at Singapore Polytechnic, a full stack developer, and an aspiring AI developer. Success means a visitor can understand that he builds across frontend, backend, databases, local AI tooling, cloud/cybersecurity coursework, and hackathon prototypes, then inspect real project evidence from GitHub.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, curious, builder-first. The voice is confident without pretending to be senior, technical without becoming unreadable, and honest about learning through shipped experiments. The experience should feel like a local AI workstation: focused, practical, slightly intense, and clearly authored by someone who likes building tools.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic portfolio templates, copied layouts from Anthony Sistilli or any other creator, fake senior positioning, vague "passionate developer" copy, AI buzzword fluff, over-polished SaaS gloss, tiny repeated eyebrow labels, and hardcoded content that should live in the CMS.

## Design Principles

1. Student credibility without apology: show the year, diploma, school, learning speed, and real build evidence plainly.
2. Ownership over aesthetics: every project needs role, what Nachiketh personally built, constraints, and future improvements.
3. Full-stack plus AI signal: make the bridge between apps, APIs, databases, local models, automation, and security obvious.
4. CMS-backed narrative: profile details, skills, projects, posts, social links, and proof points should stay editable.
5. Practical taste: the site should feel sharp and technical, but the interface must still be readable, responsive, and accessible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, semantic page structure, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, descriptive alt text, reduced-motion support, responsive layouts from small mobile to desktop, and copy that is clear for non-specialist recruiters as well as technical reviewers.
